Career Timeline

  • 1978:Turns pro in the WHA with the Indianapolis Racers before joining the Edmonton Oilers.
  • 1979:Officially enters the NHL as the Oilers join the league.
  • 1981:Records his first 50-goal season and establishes himself as an elite scorer.
  • 1984-1988:Leads Edmonton to four Stanley Cup titles in five seasons.
  • 1988:Shocked hockey fans with a blockbuster trade to the Los Angeles Kings.
  • 1994:Breaks Gordie Howe's all-time goals record.
  • 1999:Retires as the most decorated offensive player in hockey history.

Legacy

Gretzky's vision, anticipation, and playmaking transformed modern hockey. His influence extends far beyond his statistics—he expanded the game's reach across North America and inspired generations of players.

In 1999, the NHL retired his number 99 league-wide, a unique honor that reflects his singular impact on the sport.
